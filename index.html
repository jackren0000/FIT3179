Can GDP reflect military expenditure and happiness index?

Visualisations among this page show the relationship between military expenditure, GDP and happiness index

World GDP map

This map shows the distribution of GDP around the world.

Gross domestic product (GDP) is a monetary measure of the market value of all the final goods and services produced in a specific time period. It is often considered to be the "world's most powerful statistical indicator of national development and progress".

Australia, the United States, Canada and most of Europe have high GDP. South America, Asia and Africa and most developing countries have low GDP. GDP in most countries is above 20,000(per capita).

So, is it related to military expenditure ?

World military expenditure(% of GDP) map

The map shows the distribution of military expenditure around the world.

Libya, Saudi Arabia and Algeria contains the most military expenditure which is 15.48%, 7.98% and 6.01% respectively. GDP is not high in these countries. Besides, Russia, colombia and Namibia also have more than 3% military expenditure(% of GDP). None of these countries with high military spending has high GDP.

However, some Middle Eastern countries also have relatively higher GDP at higher military costs.

Does GDP correlate with happiness?

The headline result is clear: the richer the country, on average, the higher the level of self-reported happinessself-reported happiness.

From right figure, we can see a positive correlation between GDP and happiness index. However, in addition to income, there must be other important factors that can improve people's happiness.

In addition to income inequality, other possible important factors are the degree of peace and corruption. In Russia, China, India, Central Asian and Central African countries, for example, the degree of corruption plays an important role in people ' s happiness. Using GPI instead of GDP

GDP shows economic activity, measuring all goods and services produced in a given period. In this model, all economic activity contributes to GDP, so any increase in goods/services—whether it is hiring new teachers to improve education or rebuilding homes after a hurricane—positively affect GDP.

GPI looks at a combination of economic, environmental, and social indicators to create a picture of the overall progress of a country or state. GPI also starts with measuring economic activity but includes negative economic factors such as income inequality and the cost of underemployment.

The welfare of a nation can scarcely be inferred from a measure of national income. If the GDP is up, why is America down? Distinctions must be kept in mind between quantity and quality of growth, between costs and returns, and between the short and long run. Goals for more growth should specify more growth of what and for what.
